Kalman Filter

The Kalman Filter is an algorithm for estimating the state of a system in the presence of uncertainty. The algorithm was developed by the Hungarian-American engineer Rudolf E. Kálmán and is ubiquitous today in applications such as robotics, navigation and control.

One of the biggest advantages of the Kalman Filter is its ability to combine measurements from multiple different sensors into one model.

The goal of this website is to offer an interactive playground in order to gain a better intuition for the effects of the algorithm.

Our Application

We will look at the following simplified use case for a Kalman Filter: A car is driving in one direction with changing velocity v over a period of time. We are trying to predict the current position of the car at all times. However we only periodically receive measurements of the current velocity from the speedometer and the current position from a GPS sensor. Moreover, both sensors are imperfect and deliver noisy data.
